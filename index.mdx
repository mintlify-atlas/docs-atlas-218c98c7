Etcher is a free, open-source desktop application that makes flashing OS images to SD cards and USB drives simple, safe, and reliable. Whether you're setting up a Raspberry Pi, installing a Linux distro, or deploying a custom OS image, Etcher handles it all with built-in verification to ensure every byte is written correctly.

## Why Etcher?

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Safe by default" icon="shield-check">
    Etcher automatically filters out system drives to protect your data from accidental overwrites
  </Card>
  <Card title="Verified writes" icon="circle-check">
    Every flash is followed by a verification pass that confirms every byte was written correctly
  </Card>
  <Card title="Cross-platform" icon="display">
    Runs natively on Windows 10+, macOS 10.13+, and most Linux distros
  </Card>
  <Card title="Auto-decompression" icon="file-zipper">
    Flash compressed images directly — no need to extract ZIP, GZ, or XZ files first
  </Card>
  <Card title="Multi-drive flashing" icon="copy">
    Write the same image to multiple drives simultaneously in a single operation
  </Card>
  <Card title="Wide format support" icon="layer-group">
    Supports ISO, IMG, DMG, VHD, ZIP, GZ, XZ, and many more image formats
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Get started in minutes

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download Etcher">
    Visit [balena.io/etcher](https://balena.io/etcher) and download the installer for your operating system
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select your image">
    Click **Flash from file** to choose a local image, or use **Flash from URL** to download one directly
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select your drive">
    Click **Select target** and choose your SD card or USB drive — system drives are hidden for safety
  </Step>
  <Step title="Flash and verify">
    Click **Flash!** and Etcher will write and then verify your image automatically
  </Step>
</Steps>
