---
title: "Quick start"
description: "Flash an OS image to an SD card or USB drive in a few steps."
---

This guide walks you through flashing your first image with Etcher. The entire process takes only a few minutes.

<Note>
  Make sure Etcher is installed before you begin. See the [Installation](/installation) guide if you haven't set it up yet.
</Note>

## Flash your first image

<Steps>
  <Step title="Launch Etcher">
    Open Etcher from your Applications folder (macOS), Start menu (Windows), or application launcher (Linux).

    You'll see the main interface with three sections: source image, target drive, and the **Flash!** button.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select your source image">
    Click **Flash from file** to choose an image stored on your computer, or click **Flash from URL** to have Etcher download the image directly from a web address.

    Etcher supports a wide range of formats including ISO, IMG, DMG, and VHD. If your image is compressed (ZIP, GZ, BZ2, or XZ), Etcher decompresses it automatically — no need to extract it first.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select your target drive">
    Click **Select target** to open the drive picker.

    <Warning>
      Only removable drives appear in the list. Etcher automatically hides your system drives and internal hard disks to prevent accidental data loss. If your drive does not appear, check that it is properly connected and mounted.
    </Warning>

    Select your SD card or USB drive from the list and click **Select**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Flash!">
    Click the **Flash!** button to start the process.

    Etcher may ask for your administrator or system password — this is required to write directly to the drive. Enter your credentials and confirm.

    The progress bar shows the write status in real time. Do not remove the drive while flashing is in progress.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Wait for verification to complete">
    After writing finishes, Etcher automatically reads the drive back and verifies that every byte matches the source image. This step confirms the flash was successful.

    When verification passes, Etcher displays a **Flash Complete!** message. Your drive is ready to use.

    <Tip>
      If verification fails, try reflashing with a different USB port or a fresh copy of the image. A failed verification often indicates a faulty drive or a corrupted download.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What to do next

Your drive is now ready. Remove it safely from your computer and boot your device from it.
